Here's a list of things you need to do to be elligble to volunteer

  1. Be at least 18 years old.
  2. Attend required training.
  3. Have access to transportation.
  4. Complete a foster care application and sign a foster care agreement.
  5. Be able to set aside time (as often as every two weeks) to bring your foster animal in for follow-up vaccinations and spay/neuter surgery
  6. Have valid id preperad (Here's a list of valid forms of id)
    • Drivers license
    • Birth Certificate
    • State-issued Identification Card.
    • Social Security Card.
    • Passport or Passport Card.
  7. Try to spend a minimum of two hours per day with kittens to socialize them and monitor their health (time commitments will vary depending on your foster animals).
  8. Existing animals in your home must be up-to-date on vaccines and in good health
  9. Every family member in the household will need to be prepared for the commitment and emotions involved with fostering.
  10. Kitten/puppy-proof your home to reduce potential safety hazards
  11. Quarantine your foster animals for 14 days to prevent disease spread, as required
  12. Ensure that animals can be fostered in your home
  13. Ensure that animals can be fostered in your home
  14. Maintain communication with the Foster Program Coordinator
  15. Maintain a peaceful, loving environment for the foster animals and spend quality time socializing them
  16. Commit to the entire foster period with the animal
  17. Utilize the emergency contact number for after-hour emergencies
